Method  overloading
-----------------------
1) What  is  method  overloading ?  --->  Same  method  does  different  operations

2) In  other  words,  adding  a  new  feature  to  same  method   is  known  as  method  overloading

3) What  does  add(10 , 20)  do ?  --->  Returns  sum  of  two  numbers  i.e.  30
    What  does  add("10" , "20")  do ?  ---> Returns  join  of  two  strings  i.e.  "1020"

4) What  is  the  key  to  execute  overloaded  methods  ?  --->  
										Type  of  argument  plays  a  key  role  in  execution  of  the  method

5) What  does  add()  method  do  when  numbers  are  passed  to  the  method ?  --->  Returns  sum  of  two  numbers
     What  does  add()  method  do  when  strings  are  passed  to  the  method ?  ---> Returns  join  of  two  strings

6) Same  add()  method  does  either  sum  operation  (or)  join  operation  based  on  arguments

7) Adding  another  functionality  to  same  method  is  known  as  method  overloading

8) class  c1
    {
        void   m1(int  x)
	{
	}
   }
   class  c2
   {
       void  m1(double  x)
       {
       }
   }
   Is  m1()  method  overloaded ? ---> No  becoz  it  is  in  two  different  classes

9) In  other  words,  overloaded  methods  should  be  in  the  same  class

10) Method  overloading  is  nothing  but  code  refinement

11) Method  overloading  is  also  called  static  polymorphism  (or) compile  time  polymorphism

12) What  is  polymorphism ?  --->   Many  forms  i.e. same  method  has  got  different  forms

13) add()  method  has  got  6  different  forms
      i.e.  add(int , int) , add(int , int , int) , add(double , double) , add(String , String) , add(float , float) , add(char , char)

14) What  is  the  advantage  of  method  overloading ? --->  Method  calls  becomes  simple  and  readable
											       i.e.  Program  readability  increases

15) Can  main()  method  be  overloaded ?  --->  Yes

16) What  is  the  1st  rule  of  method  overloading ?  --->  Two  (or)  more  methods  can  have  same  name
      What  is  the  2nd  rule  of  method  overloading ?  ---> Signatures  of  methods  should  be  different

17) In  other  words,  methods  can  have  same  name  with  different  signatures

18) What  is  a  signature  ?  ---> Types  of  arguments

19) What  happens  when  two  methods  have  got  same  name  and  signature ?  --->
													Ambiguity  error  and  program  won't  be  compiled

20) Therefore  types  of  arguments  should  be  different  between  the  methods
